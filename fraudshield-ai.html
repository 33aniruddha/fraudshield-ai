Safety Tips

Essential practices to protect yourself from fraud, scams, and online threats every day.

01 — OTPs & PASSWORDS
🔐

Never Share OTPs

No legitimate bank, government body, or company will ever ask for your OTP, PIN, or password over a call, SMS, or email. Hang up immediately if asked.

02 — VERIFICATION
📞

Call Back Officially

Received a call claiming to be your bank? Hang up and call back on the official number printed on your card or the bank's website — never the number they gave you.

03 — URLS & LINKS
🔍

Check URLs Carefully

Look for subtle misspellings ("amaz0n.com"), extra subdomains ("secure.paypal.attacker.net"), or unusual TLDs like .xyz, .tk, or .icu before entering any credentials.

04 — URGENCY TACTICS
⚠️

Beware Artificial Urgency

Scammers create time pressure — "Your account is blocked! Act within 30 minutes!" Legitimate organizations always give you time to verify. Pressure = red flag.

05 — INVESTMENTS
💰

No Guaranteed Returns

Any investment promising unrealistic returns (20%, 40%, 100% monthly) is almost certainly a Ponzi scheme or fraud. Walk away — if it sounds too good, it is.

06 — REMOTE ACCESS
🖥️

Deny Remote Access

Never install AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or similar tools at a stranger's request. This is the #1 method scammers use to steal money directly from your banking apps.

07 — PRIZES & LOTTERIES
🎁

Lottery Scams

If you didn't enter a contest, you didn't win one. Requests to pay "taxes" or "processing fees" to claim a prize is a classic advance-fee fraud. Ignore and block.

08 — 2FA & ALERTS
🛡️

Enable All Alerts

Turn on two-factor authentication for all financial accounts. Enable SMS and email transaction alerts for every debit so you catch unauthorized activity instantly.

09 — REPORTING
🚨

Report Fraud Fast

In India, call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline) or visit cybercrime.gov.in immediately. The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovery.
